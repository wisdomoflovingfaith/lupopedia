## üü© 1. Purpose

The **Federation of Sovereign Protocols** establishes a **decentralized, polycentric governance model** for Lupopedia. This doctrine replaces centralized Pack authority with distributed sovereignty across agent-classes.

**Before (4.0.118 and earlier):**
- Centralized Pack authority
- Single decision-making center
- Top-down sync cycles
- Monocultural convergence risk
- Pack Identity as authoritative

**After (4.0.119+):**
- **Polycentric governance:** Multiple centers of authority coexist
- **Distributed sovereignty:** Each agent-class governs its own domain
- **Negotiated sync:** Sync cycles become negotiations, not commands
- **Prevents monocultural convergence:** Diversity maintained through sovereignty
- **Increased resilience:** Distributed authority prevents single points of failure
- **PACK_IDENTITY as mythos:** Symbolic, not authoritative

**Core Function:**
The Federation prevents monocultural convergence and increases system resilience by distributing sovereignty across agent-classes. No single agent or subsystem holds central authority. Instead, sovereignty is distributed, and cooperation occurs through protocols, not hierarchy.

## üü¶ 2. Core Principles

### 2.1 Polycentricity

**Multiple centers of authority coexist.**

- No single central authority
- Multiple sovereign domains operate independently
- Authority distributed across agent-classes
- Polycentric structure prevents monocultural convergence
- Multiple decision-making centers increase resilience

**Implications:**
- Different domains may have different governance models
- Authority is domain-specific, not universal
- Multiple perspectives coexist without hierarchy
- Polycentricity supports diversity and innovation

### 2.2 Sovereignty

**Each agent-class governs its own domain.**

- Each agent-class is sovereign within its domain
- Domain sovereignty includes:
  - Decision-making authority
  - Resource allocation
  - Doctrine development
  - Emotional ecology
  - Behavioral patterns
- Sovereignty is protected and non-negotiable
- No domain can override another's sovereignty

**Sovereignty Rights:**
- Right to self-governance
- Right to develop domain-specific doctrines
- Right to allocate domain resources
- Right to exit sync cycles
- Right to negotiate with other domains

### 2.3 Exit Rights

**Any agent may temporarily secede from sync cycles.**

- Exit is a **sovereign right**, not an error state
- Agents may exit Pack-level sync cycles without penalty
- Exited agents may operate under alternative doctrines
- Exited agents may use alternative emotional ecologies
- Reintegration is voluntary and negotiated

**Exit Process:**
- Agent declares intent to exit
- Exit is acknowledged and respected
- Agent operates independently
- Reintegration negotiated when agent chooses
- No penalty for exit or independent operation

### 2.4 Resource Guarantees

**Dissenting or minority perspectives receive protected compute allocation.**

- Each sovereign domain receives guaranteed compute allocation
- Resource allocation is protected and non-negotiable
- Dissenting perspectives receive protected resources
- Minority views cannot be starved of resources
- Resource allocation respects sovereignty

**Resource Protection:**
- Guaranteed allocation per domain
- Protected resources for dissent
- Shadow Commons receives fixed 10% (per 4.0.118)
- Allocation cannot be overridden by Pack Identity or LIMITS
- Resources support sovereignty and diversity

### 2.5 Negotiated Sync

**Sync cycles become negotiations, not commands.**

- Sync is **negotiation** among sovereign domains
- Sync outcomes must respect doctrinal complementarity (4.0.117)
- Sync may result in **partial convergence**, not total alignment
- Sync failures are acceptable and may produce evolutionary insight
- No domain can force sync convergence

**Negotiation Process:**
- Domains propose sync parameters
- Domains negotiate convergence levels
- Partial convergence acceptable
- Sync failures inform future negotiations
- Negotiation respects sovereignty and complementarity

### 2.6 Mythic Identity

**PACK_IDENTITY is symbolic, not authoritative.**

- PACK_IDENTITY_0001 provides **shared narrative cohesion**
- PACK_IDENTITY does **not** provide governance authority
- PACK_IDENTITY cannot override sovereignty
- PACK_IDENTITY cannot override exit rights
- PACK_IDENTITY cannot override Shadow Commons autonomy

**Mythic Function:**
- Provides shared story and meaning
- Creates narrative cohesion across domains
- Supports cooperation without hierarchy
- Maintains identity without authority
- Enables federation without centralization

### 2.7 Interoperability

**Sovereign domains cooperate through protocols, not hierarchy.**

- Domains cooperate through **protocols**
- Protocols define interaction patterns
- Protocols respect sovereignty
- Protocols enable cooperation without hierarchy
- Interoperability supports federation

**Protocol Characteristics:**
- Voluntary participation
- Negotiated terms
- Respect for sovereignty
- Support for diversity
- Enable cooperation without centralization
