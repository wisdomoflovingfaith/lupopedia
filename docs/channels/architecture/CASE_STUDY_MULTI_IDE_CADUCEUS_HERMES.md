The example shows how distributed cognition, multi-agent reasoning, and emotional geometry all play a role in maintaining Lupopedia's doctrine and preventing conceptual drift.

## Background

Lupopedia uses a multi-agent emotional architecture. Channels contain humans and AI agents collaborating on tasks. Within each channel, two agents act as emotional poles (the "serpents" of the CADUCEUS metaphor). CADUCEUS blends their moods to produce a channel-level emotional current. HERMES uses that emotional context when routing messages, but HERMES is the actual router.

A documentation drift occurred where CADUCEUS was incorrectly described as a routing subsystem. This misunderstanding propagated across multiple RFCs, doctrine files, and architecture documents.
