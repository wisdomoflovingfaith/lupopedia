## **1. Rule: Names Are Functional Identifiers Only**

### **What Mythic Names Are**

Mythic names in Lupopedia are:
- **Agent identifiers** ‚Äî "THOTH" identifies the truth-comparison agent
- **System component labels** ‚Äî "ANUBIS" identifies the orphan-repair system
- **Role mnemonics** ‚Äî "LILITH" identifies the boundary-testing agent
- **Functional descriptors** ‚Äî "AGAPE" identifies the emotional-context analyzer

### **Why Mythic Names Work**

Mythic names are used because:
- They are **memorable** ‚Äî Easier to remember than "Agent_Truth_Comparator"
- They **encode roles** ‚Äî The name itself suggests the function
- They **carry symbolic clarity** ‚Äî Ancient metaphors for cognitive functions
- They **avoid domain lock-in** ‚Äî Not tied to any specific technology or domain
- They are **culturally neutral metaphors** ‚Äî Universal human understanding
- They **prevent confusion** ‚Äî Distinct from technical terms

### **The Origin Story (Not the Destination)**

The creator studied 22+ religions and mythic systems to understand how humans encode cognitive functions into stories. This research informed the naming choices.

**However:**
- The research was about **understanding human cognition**, not adopting beliefs
- The names are **metaphors**, not requirements
- The system is **domain-neutral**, not religious
- The architecture is **secular**, not spiritual
